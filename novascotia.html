Nova Scotia Immigration Programs

Discover pathways to permanent residency through the Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP) and Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP).

Immigrate to Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia offers several immigration pathways for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs looking to settle in Canada's ocean playground.

Why Choose Nova Scotia?

Nova Scotia offers a high quality of life with affordable housing, excellent healthcare, world-class education, and stunning natural beauty. The province has a thriving economy with opportunities in healthcare, IT, ocean technology, and more.

Nova Scotia Immigration Programs

Nova Scotia Experience: Express Entry

For temporary foreign workers with at least 1 year of skilled work experience in Nova Scotia. Requires a valid Express Entry profile.

Nova Scotia Labour Market Priorities

Targets specific occupations in demand. Candidates must have an Express Entry profile and meet the criteria of specific draws.

Learn More

Physician Stream

For physicians offered employment by Nova Scotia Health Authority or IWK Health Centre. No job offer needed for family physicians.

Learn More

Entrepreneur Stream

For experienced business owners/entrepreneurs who want to establish a business in Nova Scotia. Requires $150,000 net worth and $150,000 investment.

Learn More

International Graduate Entrepreneur Stream

For international graduates who want to start a business in Nova Scotia. Requires graduation from a Nova Scotia university/college.

Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)

How to Apply for Nova Scotia Nomination

1

Determine Eligibility

Check which NSNP stream matches your qualifications and experience.

2

Gather Documents

Prepare all required documents including language tests, educational credentials, and work references.

3

Submit Application

Apply to the NSNP when the stream is open. Some streams require an Expression of Interest.

4

Receive Nomination

If approved, you'll receive a provincial nomination which gives you 600 additional CRS points.

5

Apply for PR

Submit your permanent residence application to IRCC within 6 months of nomination.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the processing time for Nova Scotia PNP?

NSNP processing times vary by stream but typically take 3-6 months for nomination. After nomination, federal processing takes an additional 6-12 months.

Do I need a job offer for Nova Scotia PNP?

Most NSNP streams require a valid job offer from a Nova Scotia employer, except the International Graduate Entrepreneur Stream and Physician Stream (for family physicians).

What is the minimum CRS score needed for Nova Scotia?

Nova Scotia typically invites candidates with CRS scores between 300-500, depending on the stream and current labor market needs.

Can I apply if I'm already in Canada?

Yes, many NSNP streams accept applicants already in Canada, particularly the Nova Scotia Experience: Express Entry stream for those with Nova Scotia work experience.

How often does Nova Scotia hold PNP draws?

Nova Scotia conducts draws throughout the year, typically every 1-2 months, depending on program quotas and labor market needs.
