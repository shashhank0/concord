Canada Super Visa

Bring your parents and grandparents to Canada for extended visits with the Parent and Grandparent Super Visa program.

Super Visa Canada

The Super Visa allows parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents to visit for up to 5 years at a time.

Longer Stays

Super Visa holders can stay in Canada for up to 5 years per visit, compared to 6 months with a regular visitor visa.

Multiple Entries

The Super Visa is valid for up to 10 years and allows for multiple entries into Canada during that period.

Medical Insurance

Applicants must have Canadian medical insurance coverage for at least 1 year with $100,000 emergency coverage.

Super Visa Requirements

  • The applicant must be the parent or grandparent of a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
  • A signed letter of invitation from the child or grandchild in Canada
  • Proof that the child/grandchild meets the minimum necessary income (LICO)
  • Medical insurance from a Canadian company with at least $100,000 coverage
  • Immigration medical exam completed by an approved panel physician
  • Proof that the applicant will leave Canada at the end of their stay

Minimum Necessary Income (LICO)

The child or grandchild in Canada must prove they meet the minimum income requirements based on family size. For example:

  • 1 person (sponsor only): $27,514
  • 2 persons: $34,254
  • 3 persons: $42,100
  • 4 persons: $51,128

Income can be proven with Notice of Assessment, T4 slips, or employment letters.

Super Visa Application Process

Gather Documents

Collect all required documents including proof of relationship, invitation letter, financial documents, and medical insurance.

Complete Application

Fill out the application forms (IMM 5257 and IMM 5645) and pay the processing fee ($100 CAD).

Medical Exam

Complete an immigration medical exam with an approved panel physician.

Submit Application

Submit your application online or through a Visa Application Centre (VAC).

Biometrics

Provide fingerprints and photo (biometrics) if required.

Decision

Wait for processing (typically 3-4 months) and receive decision on your application.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the Super Visa valid?

The Super Visa is typically valid for up to 10 years, allowing multiple entries. However, each stay in Canada can be up to 5 years at a time. The border officer will determine the exact length of stay when you arrive in Canada.

Can I work in Canada with a Super Visa?

No, the Super Visa does not allow you to work in Canada. It is strictly a visitor visa for parents and grandparents. If you wish to work, you would need to apply for a work permit separately.

Can I extend my Super Visa stay?

Yes, you may apply to extend your stay as a visitor while in Canada. You should apply at least 30 days before your authorized stay expires. You'll need to continue meeting the requirements including maintaining valid medical insurance.

What's the difference between Super Visa and Parent Sponsorship?

The Super Visa is a temporary resident visa allowing extended visits, while Parent Sponsorship is a permanent residence program. Super Visa processing is faster (months vs. years) but doesn't lead to permanent status. Many families use the Super Visa while waiting for sponsorship applications to process.

Can I include my dependents in a Super Visa application?

No, the Super Visa is only for parents and grandparents. Each person must apply separately. Spouses and dependent children cannot be included in a Super Visa application and would need to apply for regular visitor visas.
